Samuel Langhorne Clemens

Satirist, Humorist, and Humanist

Portrait of young Samuel Langhorne Clemens, also known by his pen name Mark Twain. A man who is often misunderstood, even today, when his works are often banned from schools. He was an advocate for civil rights, women's suffrage, and peace. His works are censored due to the language, which was common for the time, but often he depicted minorities empathically as human beings.

The timeline of Mark Twain

  • 1835 - Samuel Langhorne Clemens was born in Florida, Missouri to Jane and John Marshall Clemens. His father was an attorney and a judge. Samuel was the sixth of seven children. Samuel was born two weeks after the closest approach to Earth of a famous comet called Halley's Comet.
  • 1847 - When Samuel was 11 years old when his father died of pneumonia. To help his family Samuel became a printer's apprentice and soon was writing articles and sketches for a newspaper owned by his older brother.
  • 1853 - Samuel traveled and worked in many places as a printer such as St. Louis, New York City, and Philadelphia. He also worked as a correspondent and gained a lot of experience in the newspaper business.
  • 1856 - Samuel gives his first speech. His great way of speaking earned him praise and he became instantly recognized as a great lecturer who was sought after as a speaker many years after this first speech.
  • 1857 - Works as an Apprentice River Pilot and learns the lingo "mark twain," which refers to the depth of the river for boats to safely travel through
  • 1864 - Moves to San Francisco, where he meets many other famous writers of the time.
  • 1865 - Jumping Frog published, first short story raising his profile as a writer
  • 1867 - Twain was a passenger aboard a ship to the Holy Land when he met his future brother in law who showed Twain a picture of his sister. He fell in love with her at first sight.
  • 1869 - The Innocents Abroad published, first book and bestseller
  • 1870 - Although Olivia Langdon rejected his first proposal of marriage they married this year in New York. She came from a wealthy family and introduced Twain to many famous people of the time. Their first and only male child died when he was almost two years old. They had three more daughters after this.
  • 1876 - The Adventures of Tom Sawyer is published.
  • 1884 - Becomes owner of Charles L. Webster & Co., a publishing company
  • 1885 - The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn, his greatest fiction is published
  • 1896 - Wife Livy Clemens dies after battle with a serious illness for two years
  • 1906 - Thomas Edison visits Mark Twain in his home at Redding, Connecticut, and he met the famous Indian politician, Mahatma Gandhi. During this time, Twain also lost a great deal of money because of bad investments. He diligently worked on his memoirs that became his legacy.
  • 1910 - Dies at the age of 74, Twain was born when Halley's Comet made a pass in orbit and Twain always said he would "go out with it" as well. He died following the return of the comet and his prediction came true.
